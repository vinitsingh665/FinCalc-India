What is an EMI?

An Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. Equated monthly installments are used to pay off both interest and principal each month so that over a specified number of years, the loan is paid off in full.

How is EMI Calculated?

The mathematical formula for calculating EMI is:

E = P x r x (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n - 1)
  • E is EMI
  • P is Principal Loan Amount
  • r is rate of interest calculated on a monthly basis (i.e., r = Rate of Annual interest/12/100)
  • n is loan tenure in months

Factors Affecting Your EMI

Understanding the components of your loan can help you plan better. The key factors that influence your EMI amount include:

  • Principal Amount: The total amount you borrow. A higher loan amount directly increases your EMI.
  • Interest Rate: The rate at which the lender charges interest. Even a small difference in rates can significantly impact your monthly outgo.
  • Loan Tenure: The duration for which you borrow the money. A longer tenure reduces your monthly EMI but increases the total interest paid over time. Conversely, a shorter tenure increases EMI but saves on total interest.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Does this calculator work for all types of loans?

Yes, this standard EMI calculator works for Home Loans, Car Loans, Personal Loans, and Education Loans that follow a reducing balance method.

2. Is the EMI amount fixed for the entire tenure?

Generally, yes. However, if you have a floating rate loan, your EMI may change if the repo rate or the bank's lending rate changes.

3. Can I use this for pre-payment calculations?

This tool gives you the base EMI. Pre-payments reduce your principal faster, which either reduces your tenure or your future EMIs, depending on your bank's policy.
